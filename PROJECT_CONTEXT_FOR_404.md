# Project Context for Branded 404 Page

## 1. Project Summary

From the Trunk is a story-led ecommerce storefront for authenticated, pre-loved luxury sarees. The codebase positions the brand as a curator of one-of-one sarees from private wardrobes, family trunks, and collector trunks, with each piece reviewed for fabric, condition, provenance, and wearability before being presented for a new owner.

The public experience is not framed as ordinary resale. It uses language such as "pre-loved luxury sarees," "re-stored," "story woven in silk," "next wardrobe," "product dossier," "condition graded," and "memory, not inventory." The brand wants shoppers to feel that each saree has been honored, preserved, authenticated, and prepared for a second story.

The 404 page should therefore feel like a missing chapter in a warm, editorial, heritage-led shopping journey, not like a generic technical error screen.

## 2. What Project Is Trying to Do

The project is trying to make pre-loved saree shopping feel trustworthy, premium, emotional, and easy to act on.

Key goals visible from the codebase:

- Help shoppers discover rare, authenticated sarees with story, fabric, condition, and provenance context.
- Give pre-loved and vintage sarees a second life through curation, restoration, and careful presentation.
- Make each product feel unique rather than interchangeable inventory.
- Connect past and present: the previous life of a saree and its next wardrobe.
- Encourage conscious buying by keeping beautiful textiles in circulation.
- Give people a way to sell or share meaningful sarees from their own trunks.
- Build trust through product dossiers, condition notes, policies, care guidance, secure checkout, and support links.

## 3. Problem Being Solved

The practical problem:

- Many sarees sit unused in homes, wardrobes, and trunks even though they still carry craft, value, and beauty.
- Buyers who want vintage, heirloom, or pre-loved sarees often lack trust in condition, authenticity, fit for occasion, and seller reliability.
- Ordinary resale listings can feel transactional, under-documented, or visually inconsistent.

The emotional problem:

- A saree often carries memory, identity, milestones, and family history.
- Letting it go or buying it second-hand can feel sensitive unless the experience treats the piece with respect.
- The brand reframes pre-loved buying as preservation, not compromise.

The 404 page should solve a smaller version of that same problem: when a user reaches a missing route, the experience should still feel cared for, branded, and useful.

## 4. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Women looking for distinctive pre-loved luxury sarees with story, provenance, and premium presentation.
- Buyers interested in heritage textiles, conscious fashion, vintage styling, wedding or festive wear, and one-of-one pieces.
- Shoppers who care about quality and emotional context, not just price.

Secondary audience:

- Saree owners or families who have meaningful sarees tucked away and want them to be loved again.
- Gift buyers or event shoppers looking for a piece with history.
- Customers who need help choosing by fabric, occasion, color, drape, or styling.

Audience mindset:

- They may be nostalgic, discerning, sustainability-aware, style-conscious, or emotionally attached to sarees.
- They need reassurance that "pre-loved" still means refined, trustworthy, and wearable.

## 5. Core User Journey

Common public journey:

1. User lands on the homepage and learns the brand promise: treasured sarees, thoughtfully restored, ready for a new story.
2. User browses the collection by fabric, category, color, availability, occasion, price, or sort order.
3. User opens a product dossier to inspect images, story, condition, measurements, care, provenance, and availability.
4. User adds a unique piece to the bag, where the language frames the bag as a "trunk selection."
5. User checks out through a secure purchase flow.
6. User receives packing/care context that reinforces the premium, hand-prepared experience.

Adjacent journeys:

- User reads Our Story, Our Why, Founders, How It Works, FAQ, or policies to build trust.
- User searches for sarees, fabrics, or story terms.
- User contacts the brand through WhatsApp, Instagram, email, or the connect dialog.
- User submits or sells a saree through the sell-with-us path.

A 404 page should act as a recovery point inside this journey. It should guide the user back to collection, search, story, or support without breaking the brand mood.

## 6. Key Pages and Their Purpose

- Homepage: Brand introduction, emotional story, collection discovery, fabric categories, social proof, featured pieces, and contact paths.
- Collection: Browse and filter one-of-one sarees and blouses by category, fabric, color, price, availability, and occasion.
- Product detail page: A "product dossier" for an individual piece, with story, provenance, condition, measurements, care, status, and related pieces.
- Search: Helps users find products by terms like fabric, story, and heirloom keywords.
- Cart and checkout: Frames purchase as a careful acquisition of a unique piece, with authentication, packing, and checkout reassurance.
- Our Story: Editorial book-like story of the brand, second life, memory, and conscious fashion.
- Our Why: Explains the philosophy: sarees are memory, not just fabric; buying pre-loved is a conscious choice.
- Founders / Our Team: Humanizes the brand through founder stories and the heritage plus technology mission.
- How It Works: Explains sourcing, quality control, restoration, packaging, and doorstep delivery.
- FAQ and policies: Establish trust around authentication, shipping, returns, care, payment, and selling sarees.
- Packing: Shows how pieces are prepared with tissue, muslin, story card, care notes, and sturdy packaging.
- Footer and navigation: Provide durable recovery paths to collection, story, policies, care, sell-with-us, Instagram, WhatsApp, and email.

A traditional blog was not clearly visible from the codebase. SEO guide and keyword landing pages appear to serve part of the informational content role.

Landing page themes:

## 8. Collection/Product/Service Page Understanding

Collection page:

- Presents the catalog as "Curated pre-loved sarees."
- Describes pieces as heirlooms from private wardrobes and collector trunks.
- Supports filters for category, fabric, color, price range, availability, and occasion.
- Uses empty-state copy like "No exact matches found" and "The next curated drop is being prepared."
- Suggests nearby options rather than leaving users at a dead end.

Product detail page:

- Frames an item as a "Product dossier."
- Highlights "Verified by FTT," "Condition graded," "Edition 1 of 1," and origin/provenance.
- Explains that adding a piece reserves the unique piece in the bag and final ownership is confirmed at checkout.
- Uses sold/reserved language like "This piece has found its next wardrobe" and "This piece is currently reserved by another buyer."
- Reinforces trust through authentication, muslin packing, shipping, care, story, and measurement details.

Service and support-adjacent pages:

- How It Works explains sourcing, quality control, eco-restoration, sustainable packaging, and delivery.
- Packing explains tissue wrap, muslin cloth bag, story card, care notes, and outer box.
- Sell With Us explains how custodians can submit sarees with photos, condition, provenance, and contact details.

404 implications:

- A missing product route should not sound like an ecommerce failure. It can sound like a piece has moved, been reserved, found its next wardrobe, or is no longer in the trunk.
- A missing content route can sound like a chapter has moved.
- The page should include useful recovery links: collection, search, Our Story, and contact.

## 9. FAQ Insights

FAQ and policy pages support the brand's trust posture.

Key FAQ insights:

- FTT is a curated marketplace for authenticated pre-loved luxury sarees.
- Sarees are sourced from homes across India, restored, and given a second life with a new custodian.
- Authentication includes a rigorous condition check for tears, stains, zari, fabric, and overall wearability.
- The sarees are truly pre-loved and may carry provenance, character, and prior ownership.
- Returns are allowed within a defined review window when an item is significantly different from its description.
- Shipping is PAN-India only; international shipping was not clearly visible as supported.
- Packaging uses tissue wrap, a recycled saree cloth or muslin-style bag, brand box, and care/provenance materials.
- Sellers can submit sarees with photos, condition details, and provenance.
- Care guidance emphasizes dry cleaning, breathable muslin or cotton storage, and avoiding plastic, sunlight, humidity, and perfume.

For the 404 page, FAQ insights suggest the page can confidently route users to:

- Browse the collection.
- Learn what pre-loved means.
- Read authentication/condition details.
- Contact support if they reached the page from an order, product, or policy concern.

## 11. Brand Essence

Brand essence in one sentence:

From the Trunk helps meaningful sarees leave the trunk, keep their memory, and find a new wardrobe with trust, beauty, and care.

Brand pillars:

- Heritage: sarees carry craft, family history, and cultural memory.
- Trust: authentication, condition grading, product dossiers, policies, and support.
- Circularity: keeping existing textiles in circulation instead of making everything new.
- Editorial beauty: premium photography, serif headings, gold details, and story-led presentation.
- Human care: restoration, careful packing, founder narrative, and direct support.

Brand tension:

- It must feel luxurious without feeling wasteful.
- It must feel pre-loved without feeling worn-out or second-rate.
- It must feel emotional without becoming sentimental in a heavy-handed way.

## 12. Tone of Voice

The voice is warm, refined, evocative, and reassuring.

Common voice characteristics:

- Story-led: "chapter," "story," "memory," "journey," "wardrobe."
- Careful and tactile: "hand," "muslin," "folded," "wrapped," "drape," "woven."
- Trust-oriented: "authenticated," "condition graded," "documented," "verified."
- Heritage-aware: "private wardrobes," "family trunks," "heirloom," "craft."
- Calm and premium: not loud, not bargain-heavy, not playful in a childish way.

404 voice direction:

- Avoid saying only "404" or "page not found" as the emotional headline.
- Use a branded line first, then a clear explanation.
- Suggested tone: "This piece isn't in the trunk" or "That chapter has moved."
- Support copy should be simple and useful: the page may have moved, the piece may no longer be available, or the story may have been archived.

## 18. What the 404 Page Should Achieve

The 404 page should:

- Preserve the premium brand mood.
- Clearly explain that the page, product, or chapter could not be found.
- Avoid making the user feel they made a mistake.
- Offer immediate recovery paths.
- Reinforce the brand idea of trunk, story, care, and second life.
- Invite continued discovery rather than forcing a dead end.
- Work well on mobile, since the storefront appears mobile-conscious.
- Use real brand language and visual symbols rather than generic web-error language.

Minimum useful content:

- Branded eyebrow such as "404" or "Missing chapter."
- Main headline using the trunk/story metaphor.
- Short explanation.
- Primary CTA to collection.
- Secondary CTA to home or story.
- Optional search/fabric chips.
- Optional support contact if a user expected an order, product, or policy page.

## 19. 404 Storyline Concepts

### Concept 1: The Missing Chapter

Core idea:

The user has opened the story book, but this chapter has moved or is no longer on the page.

Visual idea:

- Open book or product dossier.
- One page missing or gently lifted away.
- A silk thread or saree border leading toward the collection.
- Gold page marker or ribbon with "404."

Best fit:

- Missing story pages, guide pages, founder/team content, policy links, or editorial routes.

Suggested headline:

"That chapter has moved."

Suggested support copy:

"The story you were looking for is not on this page anymore. The trunk still has other pieces waiting to be discovered."

### Concept 2: Not in the Trunk

Core idea:

The user searched inside the trunk, but the specific page or piece is not there.

Visual idea:

- Open burgundy vintage trunk on ivory background.
- Folded sarees visible inside.
- One empty slot, small story card, or embroidered "404" tag.
- Gold keyhole, arch, and floral line art behind it.
- A fabric trail leading toward the CTAs.

Best fit:

- General 404 page.
- Missing collection/product routes.
- Brand-level default error recovery.

Suggested headline:

"This piece isn't in the trunk."

Suggested support copy:

"The page may have moved, or the piece may have already found its next wardrobe. Explore the collection to find another story in silk."

### Concept 3: The Saree Found Its Next Wardrobe

Core idea:

If a missing route was related to a product, the brand can imply the one-of-one piece may no longer be available.

Visual idea:

- Empty hanger or product card slot.
- Muslin bag and story card left behind.
- Small "found its next wardrobe" note.
- Soft gold border and burgundy detail.

Best fit:

- Missing product pages, discontinued pieces, or archived one-of-one inventory.

Suggested headline:

"This saree may have found its next wardrobe."

Suggested support copy:

"Every piece here is one of one. Browse similar chapters from the current collection."

## 20. Recommended 404 Illustration Direction

Recommended direction:

"Open Trunk, Missing Piece."

This is the strongest direction because it uses the brand name, existing footer asset language, product inventory model, and current not-found copy.

Rough art direction:

- Warm ivory background.
- Deep burgundy vintage trunk open at a soft three-quarter angle.
- A few folded sarees or silk borders visible inside.
- One intentionally empty space where a saree or story card should be.
- Gold hardware, keyhole, and delicate arch/floral line work.
- A small "404" placed as an embroidered tag, product dossier card, or luggage label rather than a huge web-error number.
- A fabric thread or pallu edge subtly points toward the action area.
- Mood: calm, refined, wistful, useful.

Color guidance:

- Use burgundy `#601d1c`, gold `#b39152`, ivory `#fdf7f1`, navy `#141d46`, and soft border tones.
- Keep contrast accessible for copy and buttons.

Do not create:

- Cartoon mascot.
- Broken robot or glitch screen.
- Generic browser window illustration.
- Overly dark abandoned attic mood.
- Cheap clearance-sale styling.
- Dense illustration that competes with navigation and CTAs.

## 21. Reusable Empty-State Concepts

### General 404

- Message: "This piece isn't in the trunk."
- Visual: open trunk with missing story card.
- Action: Browse the Collection, Return Home.

### No Search Results

- Message: "No curated pieces matched this search."
- Visual: magnifying glass over folded silk or product dossier cards.
- Action: Try chips such as Banarasi, Kanjeevaram, Silk, Heirloom, or browse all.

### Empty Collection or Filter Result

- Message: "This edit is waiting for the right pieces."
- Visual: labeled empty fabric tray inside the trunk.
- Action: Reset filters, explore newest pieces, or browse nearby fabric/occasion edits.

### Product Unavailable or Archived

- Message: "This piece has found its next wardrobe."
- Visual: empty product card slot, folded muslin bag, story card, or sold tag.
- Action: Explore similar pieces.

### Missing Story or Guide

- Message: "That chapter has moved."
- Visual: open book with a ribbon marker and one lifted page.
- Action: Read Our Story or browse guides if available.

### Broken or Missing Product Image

- Message: "The photograph is being restored."
- Visual: gold-framed image placeholder with textile texture.
- Action: Keep product details visible and let the user continue browsing.

### Packaging Media Empty

- Message: "Packaging photos coming soon."
- Visual: wrapped box or muslin bag outline.
- Action: Link to care and packing details if available.

## 24. What to Avoid

Avoid:

- Generic "Oops, page not found" as the main branded idea.
- Technical language like route, server, request, backend, status code, or broken URL in user-facing copy.
- Glitch graphics, broken robot illustrations, warning signs, or browser-window error art.
- Childish cartoon treatment.
- Cheap resale, thrift-bin, clearance, or discount-heavy framing.
- Aggressive urgency or false scarcity beyond the real one-of-one model.
- Saying a missing product will return unless the system actually supports that promise.
- Inventing certifications, press mentions, review counts, or authenticity guarantees not visible in the codebase.
- Overly sad, abandoned, dusty, or haunted-trunk storytelling.
- Over-cluttering the page with too many links.
- Exposing private order, account, backend, CMS, environment, or operational details.
- Copy that blames the user.
- A style that feels like a SaaS dashboard empty state instead of a luxury saree brand.
